What is a Sprain?
Sprains are stretching injuries of ligaments that connect muscles to bones. Ligaments are connective tissues that hold bones together in the skeleton. Sprains can occur at any joint where two bones are connected. The severity of the injury may vary from a minor stretching that may soon heal fully to a major tear that makes the bony link unstable (1✔



).
Sprains are classified into 3 categories:
Severe
Partial
Mild

Common Causes of Sprains
Sprain can be caused by either a direct or indirect injury that can force a joint to an unnatural position, overstretches, and torn ligaments (2✔



).
Running or walking on uneven ground
Wearing high-heeled shoes
Overstretching of a ligament
During a fall
Lifting heavy weight
Accidents
